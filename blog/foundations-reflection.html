Foundations — Human Skills Reflection

Journal prompt

What surprised me the most about the Human Skills learning is...

I was surprised how much practising small things, like a short mindfulness break or better listening. This actually changes how I work and feel. I expected human skills to be a little add-on, but they made technical tasks easier because I could focus better and ask for help more clearly.

Reflection Questions

Three new things I learned about myself and my ego

  1. I need time to understand things well. I learned that taking an extra minute or two to re-read concepts helps me build solid knowledge rather than rushing.
  2. My shyness affects asking for help. I sometimes hesitate to speak up when there are many people, so I need to practice asking for help early and in small steps.
  3. I feel proud when I fix my own mistakes. Going back over my code and finding the error makes me more confident and patient with my learning process.

Role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning & programming

Values guide the choices I make (how I treat teammates, what I prioritise). Empathy helps me understand other people's ideas and communicate clearly. Self-awareness helps me recognise when I'm stuck, tired, or stressed and take the right break or ask for help. Together they make group work smoother and learning faster.

What surprised me most

That practicing simple habits (5 minutes of mindful breathing, or a structured listening loop) actually made me less reactive and more able to focus on hard problems.

Most challenging parts of Human Skills

The hardest part for me was actually doing the practices consistently (mindfulness every day) and being brave enough to ask for help in front of a group. I’m shy when many people are around.

Why Human Skills in a coding bootcamp?

Technical skill gets you a job; human skills help you keep it and grow in a team. Employers value teamwork, communication, leadership and the ability to manage stress, all are built by Human Skills practice. For me, they help me learn faster and make me a better teammate.

Was this time a waste? Should I have used it to practise code instead?

Not a waste. The Human Skills time would be an investment. After practising these skills I would be able to focus, communicate, and recover from stress better which would make my coding time more efficient. Doing both (human skills + coding) would be the best approach.

Short action plan

  • Daily: 5-minute mindfulness after breakfast (or a short walk when stressed).
  • Ask for help early: message a classmate or facilitator; if in a group, ask one person privately first.
  • Weekly: 20 minutes on Sundays to update the Learning Plan and reflect on progress.
