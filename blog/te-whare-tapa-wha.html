Introducing Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984. It represents four dimensions of health: physical, mental, spiritual, and family wellbeing. The model uses the metaphor of a wharenui (meeting house) with four walls, each wall representing a different aspect of wellbeing.

The Four Dimensions

  • Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing): How you care for your body through activity, nutrition, and rest.
  • Taha Hinengaro (Mental & Emotional Wellbeing): Your thoughts, feelings, and mindset.
  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing): Your sense of purpose, connection, and faith.
  • Taha Whānau (Family & Social Wellbeing): Your relationships with family, friends, and community.

Wellbeing Plan Example

Here is a simple plan to support each dimension:

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana Going for a walk or swim twice a week
Taha Whānau Having dinner with my family every evening
Taha Hinengaro Practicing mindfulness or meditation daily
Taha Wairua Taking time for reflection or gratitude each day
Whenua Walking barefoot in nature weekly
